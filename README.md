## Business Context

CloudTask is a Software as a Service company that delivers business metrics performance tracking for a variety of industries including finance, education, real estate and many others. Over the past four years, CloudTask has experienced slow revenue growth alongside a high rate of customer churn.

This project aims to analyze customer churn trends and their impact on the company's business from 2022 to 2025 by evaluating the following metrics:

* Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) – the monthly revenue generated from active users
* Churned MRR – the monthly revenue lost due to customer churn
* Churn Rate – the percentage of customers lost over the given period (2022–2025)

 ## Business Problems

* The number of churned customers has increased as the customer base grew over the years, with the churn rate remaining at a concerning 33.64% to 39.72% annually.
* Cloudtask has lost a total of 313 customers between 2022 to 2025, more than half of the customers acquired during the same period.
* This issue is causing the annual revenue losses to increase significantly, rising from $35K in 2022 to $122K in 2025.

## Churn Profile

* The high churn rate is observed across all regions and industries at a rate about 40%.
  *  Customer churning is a company-wide issue and it's affecting overall CloudTask business performance.
* The Starter plan has consistently recorded the highest number of customer churn every quarter, followed by Professional, Business, and Enterprise.
  *  Indicates our starter plans have failed to provide sufficient value for customers to remain and upgrade.
* Monthly billing customers account for a higher number of churned customers than annual billing customers.
  *  However the average customer lifetime of annual billings is only 43 days longer compared to monthly.
  *  Therefore, the company's retention problem extends beyond billing frequency.

## Churned Reason

* Financial related factors influence most of the reasons for churn, with budget cuts and high pricing ranking as the top two reported reasons.
  *  At the same time, approximately 75% of customers underutilize the available product features.
  *  This suggests most of our customers pay for features they may not fully utilize the product features.
  *  As a result, customers may feel that they are paying for more than they need.
* CloudTask has received relatively low average customer ratings for four consecutive years, from 2022 to 2025, ranging from 3.82 to 5.16 among both current and churned customers.
  * Among the 470 customers who provided low ratings (scores of 1–6), 67% of them were churned.
  * This shows that dissatisfaction with product performance and/or customer support may contribute to customer attrition.
  * Therefore, it would be valuable to investigate the support tickets submitted by these customers over the past 12 months to identify the key issues contributing to dissatisfaction and churn.

## Recomendation

### Review Pricing, Product Plan & Features Strategy

Why?
* Pricing is the one of the main reasons customers churn.
* About 75% of customers pay for features they may not fully utilize.

Proposed Actions:
* Conduct a product usage analysis to identify high-demand features.
* Review the essential features used by most customers and introduce them as part of a starter plan at a more affordable cost.
* Introduce flexible plans or add-on features that allow customers to pay only for the features they need.

### Improve Customer support and Product experience

Why?
* 78% of customers give a low rating score(1-6).
* Low average rating from last 4 consecutive years

Proposed Actions:
* Conduct a root-cause analysis of support tickets from the last 12 months to identify the key issues contributing to dissatisfaction and churn.
